How It Works

Set it up once. Forget about it.

UT Instapoll Alert runs silently in the background. No extra tabs to watch, no extra logins.

1

Install the extension

Install free from the Chrome Web Store. Click the toolbar icon and enter your Instapoll Course ID. Takes under two minutes.

2

Open Instapoll and go to class

Keep polls.la.utexas.edu open in any tab. The extension monitors the page’s own network traffic — no extra requests, no credentials stored.

3

Get alerted the instant a poll opens

The moment a live UT Instapoll poll is detected, you hear an audio chime, see a desktop notification, and optionally receive a push to your phone. Click through and submit before the window closes.

FAQ

Common questions

Everything UT students ask before installing.

What is UT Instapoll?
UT Instapoll is the attendance and participation polling system used by UT Austin courses, hosted at polls.la.utexas.edu. Instructors release time-limited polls during class — typically open for 2–5 minutes — and students earn credit by responding.
Is UT Instapoll Alert free?
Yes, completely free. No subscription, no trial, no ads. Open-source software made by a UT Austin student. Inspect every line of code on GitHub.
Does it store my UT login or NetID?
No. The extension never requests, reads, or stores your UT credentials, NetID, or student ID. It only reads the API responses your browser already receives in your own authenticated session.
Does UT Instapoll Alert violate the UT Honor Code?
The extension is designed for students physically present in class who want to avoid missing a poll while taking notes. Using it to answer polls for a class you are not attending violates the UT Honor Code, and the extension explicitly discourages that use.
How do I get phone alerts for Instapoll?
Enable the ntfy.sh push option in the extension popup and enter any topic name you choose (a random string works well). Install the free ntfy app on your phone and subscribe to the same topic. Your phone will buzz the moment a poll opens.
Which browsers are supported?
Chrome and any Chromium-based browser: Microsoft Edge, Brave, and Arc all work. Firefox is not currently supported.
How is this different from just watching the Instapoll tab?
Instapoll gives no audio alert and the only visual cue is a subtle change in tab content. If you are working in another app or your laptop is muted, it is easy to miss. UT Instapoll Alert interrupts you with sound, a system notification, and optionally a phone buzz — whichever reaches you first.
